1. DECIDE EMPLOYMENT FORMAT
   ─────────────────────────
   This is the single biggest blocker. Everything else depends on it.

   Options:
   • CIFRE convention — BdF pays salary, split time between BdF and
     university. Best of both worlds but requires BdF management
     approval and a partner lab. CIFRE applications go through ANRT
     and take 2–3 months to process.
   • Part-time ("salarié-doctorant") — Keep BdF position, PhD on the
     side. Slower (4–5 years instead of 3), but no income risk.
   • Full-time leave — Maximum focus but lose salary.

   → ACTION: Talk to manager and HR this week. Ask if BdF has a PhD
     support program or existing CIFRE partnerships.
   → DEADLINE: Decision by end of April 2026.

   Concrete leads to investigate:

   • PSE — international macro group

   • Recent BdF Working Paper authors on nowcasting topics

   → ACTION: Make a shortlist, read 2–3 papers from each candidate,

   → DEADLINE: Shortlist ready by end of March 2026.
              Emails sent by mid-April 2026.

4. RUN A QUICK PROOF-OF-CONCEPT
   ─────────────────────────────
   Don't wait for the PhD to start. A small pilot study (2–3 weeks
   of work) will:
   • Test whether the idea is technically feasible
   • Give you something concrete to show potential supervisors
   • Help you decide between model architectures

   Suggested pilot:
   • Data: Public quarterly BoP data from Eurostat (France, 2000–2025)
   • Model: Simple XGBoost to nowcast the trade balance using
     standard macro indicators (GDP, PMI, exchange rates, etc.)
   • Baseline: AR(1) model
   • Deliverable: A short memo (3–5 pages) with results and charts

   → ACTION: Set up Python environment, download data, run experiment.
   → DEADLINE: Pilot completed by end of April 2026.
