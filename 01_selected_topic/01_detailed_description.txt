### Refined Topic: **AI-Enhanced Nowcasting and Backcasting of Balance of Payments: Improving Accuracy and Policy Relevance**

**Refined Description**: This PhD research would focus on developing and evaluating AI-driven models to nowcast (real-time estimation) and backcast (historical reconstruction) key components of France’s balance of payments (BoP), such as the current account (trade balance, services, income), capital account, and financial account. The study would explore how AI techniques—particularly deep learning, natural language processing (NLP), and ensemble methods—can integrate high-frequency, unstructured, and alternative data (e.g., shipping indices, financial news sentiment, satellite imagery of trade activity) to outperform traditional econometric approaches. The research would also assess the policy implications of improved BoP estimates for monetary policy, trade policy, and external sector stability in France and the Eurozone.

**Why It’s Cutting-Edge**:
- **Research Demand**: Central banks, including the Banque de France, increasingly rely on real-time data for timely policy decisions. Traditional BoP data, often released with lags, limits responsiveness. AI-driven nowcasting addresses this gap, while backcasting improves historical data accuracy, critical for trend analysis and policy evaluation.
- **Quantitative Innovation**: Combining AI with econometrics responds to the growing demand for hybrid models that balance predictive power with economic interpretability.
- **Policy Relevance**: Enhanced BoP estimates can inform France’s trade negotiations, Eurozone coordination, and responses to external shocks (e.g., energy price spikes, geopolitical disruptions).
- **Alignment with Your Background**: This builds directly on your experience backcasting BoP data, analyzing commercial trends, and using AI and econometrics, positioning you to make a significant contribution.

### Specific Research Questions

1. **How can AI models improve the accuracy of nowcasting and backcasting BoP components compared to traditional econometric methods?**
   - Sub-question: Which AI architectures (e.g., LSTMs, transformers, gradient boosting) perform best for specific BoP components like trade or capital flows?
2. **What types of high-frequency and alternative data (e.g., shipping indices, news sentiment, or satellite data) add the most value to AI-driven BoP estimates?**
   - Sub-question: How can NLP extract actionable signals from unstructured data like trade policy announcements or corporate earnings calls?
3. **How do AI-driven BoP estimates affect the uncertainty and robustness of monetary and trade policy decisions?**
   - Sub-question: Can Bayesian methods quantify the uncertainty in AI predictions to make them more policy-relevant?
4. **What are the trade-offs between predictive accuracy and economic interpretability in AI-driven BoP models?**
   - Sub-question: How can hybrid AI-econometric models (e.g., incorporating structural restrictions) bridge this gap?
5. **How generalizable are AI-driven BoP models across countries with different economic structures (e.g., France vs. Germany vs. emerging markets)?**
   - Sub-question: Can transfer learning or federated learning improve model scalability for Eurozone-wide applications?

### Proposed Methodologies

To ensure heavy quantitative analysis, the research would combine AI, econometrics, and statistical techniques, tailored to nowcasting and backcasting tasks. Here’s a detailed methodological framework:

#### 1. **Data Collection and Preprocessing**
   - **Traditional BoP Data**: Use official BoP datasets from the Banque de France, Eurostat, and IMF (e.g., monthly/quarterly trade balances, capital flows). These provide the ground truth for training and validation.
   - **High-Frequency Data**: Incorporate real-time proxies like:
     - **Trade Activity**: Baltic Dry Index, container shipping rates, or port traffic data (e.g., from UN Comtrade or MarineTraffic).
     - **Financial Flows**: Exchange rate volatility, bond yields, or equity market flows (e.g., from Bloomberg, Refinitiv).
     - **Alternative Data**: Satellite imagery of industrial activity (e.g., Planet Labs), Google Trends for consumer behavior, or social media sentiment on trade policies.
   - **Unstructured Data**: Scrape financial news, trade policy announcements, or central bank statements using APIs (e.g., NewsAPI, X posts via scraping tools).
   - **Preprocessing**: Handle missing data with imputation techniques (e.g., Kalman filtering), normalize high-frequency data, and use NLP (e.g., BERT) to convert text into sentiment scores or topic embeddings.

#### 2. **AI Modeling**
   - **Nowcasting Models**:
     - **Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs)**: Use LSTMs or GRUs to capture temporal dependencies in trade and capital flow data.
     - **Transformers**: Apply time-series transformers (e.g., Informer) for long-sequence forecasting, leveraging attention mechanisms to weigh relevant data points.
     - **Gradient Boosting**: Use XGBoost or LightGBM for feature-rich datasets, especially when combining structured and unstructured inputs.
   - **Backcasting Models**:
     - **Autoencoders**: Reconstruct historical BoP data by learning latent representations, correcting for measurement errors.
     - **Generative Models**: Explore variational autoencoders (VAEs) or GANs to simulate missing historical data points.
   - **NLP Integration**: Use pre-trained models like RoBERTa to extract trade-related signals from news or policy texts, feeding embeddings into the main forecasting model.
   - **Ensemble Methods**: Combine predictions from multiple models (e.g., LSTM + XGBoost) to improve robustness, using stacking or Bayesian model averaging.

#### 3. **Econometric Benchmarks**
   - **Time-Series Models**: Estimate vector autoregression (VAR) or dynamic factor models (DFMs) as baselines, incorporating lagged BoP components and macroeconomic indicators (e.g., GDP, inflation).
   - **Panel Data Models**: Use fixed-effects models to control for country-specific factors when comparing France to other Eurozone countries.
   - **Structural Models**: Develop small-scale DSGE models to impose economic constraints, ensuring interpretability.
   - **Causal Inference**: Apply synthetic control methods to evaluate the impact of specific data inputs (e.g., satellite imagery) on model performance.

#### 4. **Hybrid AI-Econometric Approaches**
   - Integrate economic constraints into AI models (e.g., enforcing BoP accounting identities like current account + capital account + financial account = 0).
   - Use neural networks to estimate nonlinear relationships within a structural econometric framework, as in “deep structural models.”
   - Apply Bayesian neural networks to combine AI’s flexibility with econometric uncertainty quantification.

#### 5. **Evaluation Metrics**
   - **Accuracy**: Root mean squared error (RMSE) and mean absolute error (MAE) for nowcasting and backcasting predictions.
   - **Robustness**: Test model performance during turbulent periods (e.g., 2008 crisis, COVID-19, 2022 energy shock).
   - **Policy Relevance**: Assess forecast uncertainty using confidence intervals or density forecasts, comparing AI and econometric approaches.
   - **Interpretability**: Use SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) values to identify key drivers of AI predictions, ensuring alignment with economic theory.

#### 6. **Policy Simulations**
   - Simulate how improved BoP estimates affect policy outcomes (e.g., interest rate decisions, trade policy adjustments) using counterfactual analysis.
   - Use scenario analysis to test model responses to shocks (e.g., sudden trade restrictions, currency fluctuations).
