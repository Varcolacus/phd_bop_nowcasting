### 1. **AI-Driven Nowcasting and Backcasting of Balance of Payments Components**
   - **Description**: Develop advanced AI models (e.g., deep learning, transformers, or graph neural networks) to improve the accuracy of nowcasting and backcasting balance of payments (BoP) data, focusing on trade flows, capital flows, or remittances. Investigate how these models can incorporate high-frequency, unstructured data (e.g., shipping logs, financial news, or satellite data) to enhance real-time estimates and historical reconstructions.
   - **Why It’s Relevant**: Central banks increasingly rely on real-time data for policy decisions. AI-driven approaches are transforming traditional econometric methods by handling large, noisy datasets. This topic aligns with your current work and pushes the frontier of BoP analysis.
   - **Quantitative Angle**: Use machine learning techniques alongside econometric models (e.g., VAR, panel data models) to compare predictive performance. Apply Bayesian methods for uncertainty quantification.
   - **Potential Research Questions**:
     - How can AI improve the accuracy of backcasting BoP components compared to traditional methods?
     - What are the trade-offs between interpretability and predictive power in AI-driven BoP models?
   - **Skills Leveraged**: AI, econometrics, time-series analysis, high-frequency data processing.

### 2. **Impact of Digital Currencies on Balance of Payments Dynamics**
   - **Description**: Analyze how central bank digital currencies (CBDCs) or cryptocurrencies affect BoP components, such as current account balances, capital flows, or foreign exchange reserves. Use quantitative methods to model the transmission channels (e.g., trade invoicing, cross-border payments) and simulate policy implications for France and the Eurozone.
   - **Why It’s Relevant**: CBDCs are a hot topic for central banks, with France actively exploring digital euro projects. This topic bridges monetary policy, international finance, and technological innovation, making it highly relevant for the Banque de France.
   - **Quantitative Angle**: Develop structural econometric models (e.g., DSGE models) or agent-based models to simulate CBDC adoption. Use panel data analysis to study early adopters (e.g., China’s e-CNY) and their BoP impacts.
   - **Potential Research Questions**:
     - How do CBDCs alter the velocity of capital flows in the BoP framework?
     - Can machine learning predict disruptions in BoP stability due to cryptocurrency adoption?
   - **Skills Leveraged**: Econometric modeling, simulation techniques, machine learning for scenario analysis.

### 3. **Trade Policy and Supply Chain Resilience: A Quantitative Approach**
   - **Description**: Investigate how trade policies (e.g., tariffs, export controls, or EU trade agreements) influence France’s commercial trends and BoP resilience, particularly in the context of global supply chain disruptions. Use network analysis and AI to model supply chain dependencies and their impact on trade balances.
   - **Why It’s Relevant**: Geopolitical tensions and post-COVID recovery have heightened focus on supply chain robustness. This topic is timely for France, given its role in EU trade policy and reliance on global trade networks.
   - **Quantitative Angle**: Apply graph theory to map trade networks, combined with econometric models (e.g., gravity models) to estimate policy impacts. Use machine learning to identify vulnerabilities in supply chains based on real-time data (e.g., port congestion indices).
   - **Potential Research Questions**:
     - How do trade policies affect France’s BoP stability under supply chain shocks?
     - Can AI-driven network models outperform traditional econometric approaches in predicting trade disruptions?
   - **Skills Leveraged**: Network analysis, AI, econometrics, trade data processing.

### 4. **Climate Change and Balance of Payments: Econometric Modeling of Transition Risks**
   - **Description**: Examine how climate policies (e.g., carbon taxes, EU Green Deal) and physical climate risks affect France’s BoP, focusing on trade balances, energy imports, and capital flows. Develop econometric models to quantify transition risks and assess policy trade-offs for monetary and fiscal authorities.
   - **Why It’s Relevant**: Climate change is a priority for central banks, with the Banque de France actively researching its macroeconomic impacts. This topic combines environmental economics with international finance, aligning with global research demands.
   - **Quantitative Angle**: Use panel data econometrics to analyze cross-country impacts of climate policies on BoP. Incorporate machine learning to process climate-related data (e.g., emissions, renewable energy adoption) and predict trade flow changes.
   - **Potential Research Questions**:
     - How do carbon border adjustment mechanisms affect France’s trade balance?
     - Can AI-enhanced models improve forecasts of climate-driven BoP shocks?
   - **Skills Leveraged**: Econometric modeling, machine learning, environmental data analysis.

### 5. **Heterogeneous Agent Models for Capital Flow Volatility**
   - **Description**: Study the drivers of capital flow volatility in France’s financial account using heterogeneous agent models that account for diverse investor behaviors (e.g., institutional vs. retail investors). Integrate AI to identify patterns in capital flow data and assess their implications for monetary policy.
   - **Why It’s Relevant**: Capital flow volatility is a key concern for central banks, especially in the context of global financial integration. This topic aligns with your BoP expertise and advances quantitative finance research.
   - **Quantitative Angle**: Build computational models (e.g., agent-based or heterogeneous agent New Keynesian models) to simulate capital flows. Use machine learning to detect anomalies in high-frequency financial data.
   - **Potential Research Questions**:
     - How do heterogeneous investor behaviors amplify BoP volatility?
     - Can AI-driven clustering improve the prediction of sudden stops in capital flows?
   - **Skills Leveraged**: Computational modeling, AI, time-series econometrics.

### 6. **Machine Learning for Structural Breaks in BoP Data**
   - **Description**: Develop machine learning frameworks to detect and analyze structural breaks in historical BoP data (e.g., due to policy changes, crises, or technological shifts). Combine these with econometric methods to assess their long-term impacts on France’s commercial trends and external accounts.
   - **Why It’s Relevant**: Structural breaks are critical for accurate backcasting and forecasting, and your current work makes this a natural extension. This topic is in demand as researchers seek robust methods to handle economic regime changes.
   - **Quantitative Angle**: Apply unsupervised learning (e.g., clustering, change-point detection) to identify breaks, followed by econometric analysis (e.g., threshold regression) to estimate their effects. Use causal inference techniques to attribute breaks to specific policies or shocks.
   - **Potential Research Questions**:
     - How can machine learning improve the detection of structural breaks in BoP data?
     - What are the long-term BoP impacts of identified structural breaks in France’s trade policy?
   - **Skills Leveraged**: Machine learning, causal inference, econometric analysis.
